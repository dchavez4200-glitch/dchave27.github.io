Harvesting

Our journey begins at carefully selected coffee farms around the world. Coffee cherries are hand-picked at peak ripeness, ensuring only the finest fruit makes it into your cup.

Farmers with generations of experience know exactly when each cherry has reached its optimal flavor potential. This labor-intensive process is crucial for quality.

Did you know? A single coffee tree produces only about 1-2 pounds of roasted coffee per year!

Processing & Sorting

After harvesting, coffee cherries are processed to remove the fruit and reveal the green coffee beans inside. This can be done through wet or dry methods, each affecting the final flavor profile.

Beans are then meticulously sorted by size, density, and quality. Any defective beans are removed to ensure consistency and excellence in every batch.

Processing matters: The method used can dramatically change the taste, from fruity and wine-like to clean and balanced.

Roasting

This is where the magic happens! Green coffee beans are roasted at precise temperatures (typically 370-540¬∞F) for 10-20 minutes. During this process, the beans undergo complex chemical reactions that develop over 1,000 aromatic compounds.

Our master roasters carefully monitor temperature, time, and airflow to bring out each origin's unique characteristics. From light to dark roasts, each level reveals different flavor notes.

Roast profiles: Light roasts preserve origin flavors, while darker roasts develop bolder, caramelized notes.

Grinding

Fresh grinding is essential for optimal flavor. We grind beans immediately before brewing to preserve the aromatic oils and compounds that give coffee its distinctive taste and aroma.

The grind size is carefully calibrated for each brewing method ‚Äì fine for espresso, medium for drip coffee, and coarse for French press. This ensures proper extraction and flavor balance.

Freshness counts: Coffee begins losing its flavor within 15 minutes of grinding. That's why we grind on demand!

Brewing

Our skilled baristas extract the perfect shot using precise water temperature (195-205¬∞F), pressure (9 bars for espresso), and timing. Each brewing method ‚Äì espresso, pour-over, French press ‚Äì brings out different aspects of the coffee's flavor profile.

For espresso, the ideal extraction time is 25-30 seconds, producing a balanced shot with rich crema. For other methods, timing and water contact vary to achieve the desired strength and character.

The golden ratio: We use 1:2 ratio for espresso (1g coffee to 2g liquid) for optimal extraction.
